## Ranked Interpretation

1. Most supported: NaN/unusable policy mismatch. The paper Methods say 0-600 ms NaN trials were dismissed before classification, while current outputs retain them in the main labels. The impact is condition-asymmetric, especially in noMS.
2. Subject-specific RT trimming is a separate contributor, especially for top RT deviations such as high-trim subjects in the Source Data comparison.
3. `<1%` and first-shift boundary trials remain plausible local contributors. The top-pair trial detail CSV flags small-before-kept and near-boundary cases for direct inspection.
4. Source Data row alignment is less likely than the above because the workbook parser passed the header/row sanity checks, but the Source Data still cannot prove it came from the identical intermediate trial table.
